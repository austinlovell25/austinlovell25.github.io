Bio

I am a Master's student in Computer Science at Purdue University, where I also completed my Bachelor's degree with a GPA of 3.83/4.00. I am currently pursuing my M.S. with a perfect 4.00 GPA. My research interests lie at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and high-performance computing. I have experience developing neural networks for various applications, from predicting job queue times on supercomputers to tracking facial movements for medical diagnosis. I have presented research at SC24, the largest HPC conference, and have worked as a software engineering intern at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, Indigo BioAutomation, and Matrix Design Group.

Research

My research focuses on applying deep learning and computer vision techniques to solve real-world problems. Current interests:

  • Computer Vision for Medical Applications — Developing markerless tracking methods for facial movements during speech with submillimeter accuracy for improved diagnosis of sensorimotor issues.
  • High-Performance Computing — Using hierarchical deep neural networks to predict job queue times on supercomputers, with applications to resource scheduling and optimization.
  • LLM Interpretability — Working on projects using NNSight and PyTorch to understand and interpret large language models.
